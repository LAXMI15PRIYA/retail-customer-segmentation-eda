## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes a retail customer dataset of 200 customers to identify the 
key factors influencing monthly revenue. The goal: move beyond surface-level 
metrics and find out what *actually* separates high-value customers from the rest 
— then translate that into a concrete business recommendation.

## 📊 Dataset

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `CustomerID` | Unique customer identifier |
| `Age` | Customer age |
| `Gender` | Male / Female |
| `CityTier` | Tier classification of customer's city (1, 2, 3) |
| `MonthlyIncome` | Customer's monthly income |
| `TenureMonths` | Months since customer acquisition |
| `VisitsPerMonth` | Number of visits per month |
| `AvgOrderValue` | Average value per order |
| `MonthlyRevenue` | Total monthly revenue generated by the customer |

## 🔍 Approach

**1. Univariate Analysis** — distribution of age, income, and revenue across the customer base
**2. Bivariate Analysis** — revenue by city tier, revenue by gender, revenue vs. visit frequency
**3. Correlation Analysis** — heatmap across revenue, tenure, age, and visit frequency to isolate the real drivers

## 💡 Key Findings

**🎯 Visit frequency — not tenure or age — drives revenue**
`VisitsPerMonth` correlates strongly with `MonthlyRevenue` (**r = 0.84**), while 
`TenureMonths` (r = 0.29) and `Age` (r = -0.02) show little to no relationship. 
Customers who visit more often spend more — regardless of how long they've been around.

**🎯 CityTier 3 outperforms Tier 1 and Tier 2**
Average revenue by city tier: **Tier 3: ₹9,013.82 | Tier 1: ₹8,420.35 | Tier 2: ₹7,717.74**
This is counterintuitive, since Tier 3 typically represents smaller markets — 
possibly explained by lower competition or a different product mix. Worth deeper 
investigation.

**🎯 Gender has minimal impact on spend**
Female customers average ₹8,579.97/month vs. Male at ₹8,110.24 — only a ~5% gap, 
suggesting gender is not a meaningful segmentation variable on its own.

## 📈 Business Recommendation

Since **visit frequency** — not loyalty tenure — is the strongest predictor of 
revenue, retention strategies should shift focus toward **increasing visit 
frequency** (e.g., visit-triggered rewards, personalized re-engagement
